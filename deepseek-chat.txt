Is there a maximum height restriction for certain routes/bridges?
answer: the South African National Road Traffic Act, the maximum legal height limit for any vehicle and its load on public roads is \(4.3\) meters. Loads exceeding this limit are considered "abnormal loads" and require special permits to travel. Standard Vehicle Limit: \(4.3\) meters (includes the vehicle and any load projection). Abnormal Loads: Any height exceeding \(4.3\) meters requires an abnormal load exemption permit, plus route-specific planning to clear low-hanging bridges or power lines. High Cube Containers: International "High Cube" containers loaded on a standard trailer stand at about \(4.5\) to \(4.6\) meters, which legally classifies them as abnormal loads requiring permits. Specific Mountain Passes: Certain routes enforce much lower limits due to infrastructure constraints. For example, Bain's Kloof Pass (not far from Drakenstein) restricts heavy vehicles to \(3.68\) meters.

Do heavy gensets need to be placed directly over axles (not between them)?
answer: heavy generator sets (gensets) do not need to be placed directly over axles. The South African National Road Traffic Act (Act 93 of 1996) and its regulations do not dictate where along the length of the trailer or truck bed the load sits, but rather how much weight the axles actually carry. Axle Mass Limits: Regulation 240 specifies the maximum permissible massload per axle. As long as the placement of the genset does not cause an axle to exceed its legal limits (e.g., \(9,000\,\text{kg}\) for a single axle), its placement is legal.Load Distribution: Regulation 242 states that loads must be distributed properly across the vehicle's wheels and axles. Uneven loading causes "axle overloads" or steering issues, which lead to heavy fines.Abnormal Loads: If your genset is indivisible and exceeds standard vehicle dimensions or mass limits (like weight over \(56,000\,\text{kg}\) for combination masses), the vehicle is classified as an "abnormal load". In this case, you must apply for an exemption permit in terms of Section 81, which includes a detailed approved route and specific mass distribution diagrams.
While the Act only regulates the weight on the axles, mechanical engineering logic heavily favors placing heavy point loads, such as gensets, directly over (or just ahead of) the rear axle groupings:Traction & Steering: Proper weight distribution (about \(20\%\) of the load on the drive axle) is necessary to ensure the truck-tractor maintains traction.Trailer Chassis Limits: Placing heavy gensets between axles can cause the flatbed or lowbed chassis to bow and sag in the middle if the frame cannot support the concentrated weight.
